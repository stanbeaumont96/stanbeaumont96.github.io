This page is designed to teach you about short form adjectives in Russian.

In Russian, most adjectives have a long and short form, e.g. Красивый - красив.
The short form only exists in the nomninative form and is usually placed at the end of the sentence. You will come across short form adjectives less frequently than long form adjectives.

Long form adjectives are used 'attributively' and is placed in front of the noun - for example; The famous artist lives in Moscow.

The short form is used predicatively and is placed after the noun - for example; The climate is harsh.

The long form adjective is very common in conversational Russian, although sometimes the short form must be used to convey the correct meaning
